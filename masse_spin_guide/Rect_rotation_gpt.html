Instructions
This work is an aiming/spin guide that visualizes the english (spin) induced on a cue ball as a function of the cue position at impact. The visualization and calculations are constructed from a strict side-view of the table, meaning the view represents a vertical cross-section of the ball. In lasers.js, the “aim” rays are cast from the cue tip at center/left/right offsets to find the contact point on the ball surface; in geometry.js, rayCircleIntersection performs the corresponding tangency/contact computation. The resulting multiple contact vectors are accumulated in spin.js as a moment (r×F) and rendered as the expected spin direction and magnitude (a “spin flower” on a 0–100 scale). This produces a visual analogy for center ball, as well as limited follow / draw and massé-like effects thanks to TIP_TOP / TIP_BOTTOM and CUE_ELEVATION_MAX in config.js. The ORBIT_MAX limit and the orbit motion simulate searching for a cut angle by pivoting the cue around the ball. In short, without engaging bank/kick calculations, the system acts as a “spin trainer” that builds intuition for the relationship between contact point, cut angle, and spin direction/magnitude.
