Thank you for adding the preset piqué shot dropdown menu to the UI. The feature is implemented as requested, but the resulting shots are not curving, and we believe there is a small misunderstanding of the core mechanic we would like to clarify.

Observation:

Currently, when a preset is selected from the list, the visual indicator for the strike point on the cue ball only moves left or right along the horizontal center line. It never moves vertically into the upper half of the ball. Because of this, the executed shots do not curve, even with high cue elevation.

Clarification of the Physical Principle:

The primary action that causes a piqué or massé shot to curve is striking the cue ball above its center with an elevated cue. This high point of impact is what imparts the necessary spin for the curving effect.

The left or right adjustment (side spin) is a secondary control used to influence the direction and shape of the curve, but the curve itself is initiated by the high strike point.

Requested Correction:

Could you please adjust the implementation so that when any piqué preset is selected, the visual strike point on the UI's cue ball indicator is positioned in the upper half of the ball? The preset's left or right offset should then be applied relative to this high vertical position.

This change should ensure the physics engine receives the correct parameters to produce the curved trajectories we are looking to validate.
