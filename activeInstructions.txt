We've observed that the implementation seems to have the vertical direction inverted.

Observation:

When a piqué shot preset is selected, the visual indicator on the cue ball UI correctly moves to a vertical position, but it is positioned in the lower half of the ball instead of the upper half. Consequently, the executed shot behaves like a "draw shot" (backspin) rather than a piqué shot, and the ball does not curve.

Clarification:

It appears there might be a simple coordinate system inversion. In our aiming system, a "high" strike point corresponds to a positive vertical offset value, while a "low" strike point corresponds to a negative one. The current implementation seems to be applying a negative value, causing the low placement.

Requested Correction:

Could you please invert the sign of the vertical offset being applied in the presets? The goal is to ensure that selecting a piqué preset moves the visual strike point indicator to a fixed position in the upper half of the cue ball.

This should correct the issue and allow the physics engine to generate the proper top-spin needed for the piqué curve.
